    Title survey

    A title survey provides a complete picture of a propertyâ€™s boundaries, improvements, easements, and rights-of-way. It is often required during real estate transactions to verify ownership and ensure that the property complies with title insurance and lending requirements.

    Key Components
    • Property boundaries and corners
    • Buildings, fences, and improvements
    • Easements and rights-of-way
    • Encroachments and overlaps
    • Title and deed verification

    Common uses for Title surveys

    Our Title surveying services in New Jersey are commonly used for:

    • Real Estate Transactions - Ensuring the property matches legal records before closing
    • Title Insurance - Meeting requirements for insurance coverage and lender approval
    • Development Planning - Confirming existing structures comply with legal boundaries
    • Dispute Resolution - Identifying conflicts between recorded deeds and actual use

    • Title Insurance
    • Development Planning
    • Dispute Resolution
