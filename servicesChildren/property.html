    Property Survey

    A property survey defines the exact boundaries, dimensions, and features of a parcel of land. It is one of the most common types of surveys, used by property owners, buyers, and developers to confirm lot lines, improvements, and encroachments.

    Key Components
    • Boundary lines and corners
    • Structures, driveways, and fences
    • Easements and utility locations
    • Encroachments or overlaps
    • Lot dimensions and area

    Common uses for Property surveys

    Our property surveying services in New Jersey are commonly used for:

    • Buying or Selling Land – Confirming the exact lot size and legal boundaries before closing
    • Home Improvements – Ensuring fences, additions, or driveways stay within property limits
    • Disputes – Settling boundary disagreements with neighbors through accurate data
    • Subdivision – Dividing land into smaller lots for development or sale

    Why you need a Property Survey

    Accurate Boundaries

    Ensure you know exactly where your land begins and ends.

    Legal Protection

    Transaction Confidence

    Buyers and lenders rely on property surveys for secure transactions.

    Development Readiness

    Accurate surveys are the foundation for design, construction, and permitting.
